# Edge Cases

## Late Zi Hour

This skill uses the early/late Zi convention:

- 00:00-00:59 is early Zi and belongs to the civil date.
- 23:00-23:59 is late Zi and uses the next day for the day pillar.

This is disputed. If the user was born between 23:00 and 00:59, mention the convention and consider an alternate chart if the reading is sensitive.

## Solar-Term Boundaries

Year and month pillars follow solar terms, not Lunar New Year or lunar months.

- Year pillar changes at Li Chun.
- Month pillar changes at the monthly jie terms: Li Chun, Jing Zhe, Qing Ming, Li Xia, Mang Zhong, Xiao Shu, Li Qiu, Bai Lu, Han Lu, Li Dong, Da Xue, Xiao Han.

The included script uses a compact approximation for solar-term dates in the 1900-2100 range. If the birthday is within one day of a term boundary, require a trusted calendar or ephemeris for final certainty.

## Lunar-Only Dates

Do not convert lunar dates from memory. Ask for:

- Gregorian date from a trusted calendar.
- Exact lunar year/month/day and whether the month is leap, then state that conversion must be verified.

If no Gregorian date can be obtained, do not produce a full chart.

## Unknown Birth Time

If time is unknown:

- Omit hour pillar.
- Do not make hour-pillar claims about children, late life, subordinates, or long-term output.
- Present conclusions with lower confidence.
- Use calibration to test whether possible hour ranges fit, but do not pretend to know the time.

## Approximate Birth Time

If the time is approximate and near a two-hour boundary:

- List the possible hour branches.
- Avoid hour-pillar-specific conclusions.
- Ask whether family records can confirm the time.

## True Solar Time

Civil time may differ from true solar time depending on longitude and historical time-zone practice. This matters most near hour boundaries.

Do not perform exact true-solar-time correction unless you have reliable longitude and time-zone data. Instead:

- Mention the possible shift.
- Provide both adjacent hour possibilities if the birth time is near a boundary.

## Deceased Person

If the subject is deceased:

- Ask for the year of death if analysis reaches timing.
- Do not project luck cycles beyond death as personal future events.
- Keep language respectful and non-sensational.

## Children

For minors:

- Avoid fixed life conclusions.
- Focus on temperament, learning environment, family support, and non-deterministic suggestions.

## Health

Bazi health comments are symbolic and cultural. Never diagnose. Use wording like:

- "This pattern traditionally reminds one to watch rest, heat/cold balance, and stress habits."
- "Please rely on licensed medical professionals for symptoms or treatment."

## Marriage and Fertility

Do not guarantee marriage, divorce, pregnancy, infertility, or child outcomes. Discuss relationship patterns, communication, timing pressure, and practical choices.

## Money and Investment

Do not give investment instructions. Discuss risk posture, planning habits, timing pressure, and when to be conservative.
