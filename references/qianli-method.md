# Qianli Method

## Purpose

《千里命稿》 is useful because it teaches practical reading: how to move from chart elements to concrete life themes. Its weakness, when used carelessly, is that it tempts the agent to copy fixed sayings and overfit cases.

Use it as a method layer, not a conclusion layer.

## What to Borrow

### Palace Reading

Use pillars as life domains:

| Pillar | Domain | Typical Age Lens |
|---|---|---|
| Year | ancestry, family atmosphere, early environment, public background | childhood and outer background |
| Month | parents, siblings, education/work environment, social frame | youth and career foundation |
| Day stem | self, temperament, agency | whole life |
| Day branch | spouse/close partnership, intimate home base | adult relationship |
| Hour | children, late life, long-term output, ideals, subordinates | later life and future-facing work |

Do not hard-code the age ranges. Use them as rough lenses and confirm with dayun.

### Event Reading

For concrete events, combine:

- Ten god: what kind of person/topic is activated.
- Palace: where the topic lands.
- Branch relation: clash, combination, punishment, harm, break, meeting.
- Dayun: decade environment.
- Annual year: trigger.
- Useful/unfavorable element: whether it supports or pressures the chart.

Never infer an event from only one symbol.

### Case Analogy

When a chart resembles a classical case:

1. Name the similarity.
2. Name the difference.
3. Downgrade certainty if the useful god, luck cycle, or palace target differs.

Example:

```text
This resembles the "wealth star activated by luck" pattern, but the current chart has weaker roots and a mixed officer-killing presence, so I would read it as financial responsibility and role pressure rather than simple windfall.
```

## What to Avoid

- Do not turn palace positions into deterministic biography.
- Do not say a stem or star "must" produce a fixed event.
- Do not treat old social categories as modern moral judgments.
- Do not use marriage, children, health, or death dicta literally.
- Do not ignore feedback because a classical phrase looked convincing.

## Calibration Style

Good calibration:

```text
Medium confidence: age 24-26 may have involved relocation, study/career transition, or a shift in family expectations. The reason is that the luck cycle activated the month pillar while the annual branch clashed the travel-related branch.
```

Bad calibration:

```text
At 25 you definitely moved and your father had trouble.
```

## Revision Rules

If calibration misses:

- Recheck birth time and solar-term boundary first.
- Reassess day-master strength.
- Reassess whether the useful god was selected from regulation, structure, or ordinary balancing.
- Consider whether a different school convention affects the result.
- State the revision plainly rather than defending the first reading.

## Practical Translation

Translate symbols into modern, user-useful language:

| Classical Symbol | Modern Translation |
|---|---|
| 官杀 pressure | rules, exams, authority, deadlines, reputation pressure |
| 财星 | resources, money handling, father/partner topics depending on context |
| 印星 | study, credentials, support systems, protection, recovery |
| 食伤 | expression, output, craft, children topics, autonomy |
| 比劫 | peers, competition, team dynamics, self-assertion |

Always adapt to the user's actual question and life context.
